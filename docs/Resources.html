DIAMONDS IN THE RUFF

Older Dogs, New Leashes on Life.

Meet some of our Animal Loving Friends!

We're proud to collaborate with a network of compassionate animal shelters across the region who share our commitment to giving senior dogs a second chance. These partners help us identify five featured dogs each week—those most in need due to age, time in the shelter, or health risks—so we can give them the visibility and love they deserve.

Austin Pets Alive

Austin Pets Alive! is a nonprofit animal rescue organization based in Austin, Texas, focused on saving the lives of vulnerable animals at risk of euthanasia. It operates a no-kill shelter and develops specialized programs for animals with medical or behavioral challenges. Since its founding in 2008, the organization has played a significant role in making Austin the largest no-kill city in the United States. APA! prioritizes high-risk groups such as neonatal kittens, parvo-positive puppies, and senior or injured pets. Its work includes adoption, fostering, veterinary care, and advocacy for animal welfare practices.

Austin Animal Center

Austin Animal Center (AAC) is the municipal shelter for the City of Austin and unincorporated Travis County, providing care to thousands of animals each year. As an open-intake, no-kill facility, it accepts all animals regardless of condition and maintains a live-outcome rate above 95 percent. AAC also offers adoption, foster, and rescue programs, along with animal protection and pet support services for the community.

Austin Humane Society

The Austin Humane Society is a no-kill, nonprofit animal shelter that has served the Austin community since 1952. It provides pet adoption services, spay/neuter programs, and community outreach, helping over 11,000 animals each year. The organization also runs specialized initiatives like the Community Cat Program and a Pet Food Pantry to support both animals and pet owners in need.

Learn More
PAWS Shelter of Central Texas

PAWS Shelter of Central Texas, founded in 1986, is a no-kill, nonprofit organization with campuses in Kyle and Dripping Springs, dedicated to caring for homeless, abandoned, and abused animals in the region. The shelter provides comprehensive adoption services, including medical care, vaccinations, microchipping, and spay/neuter procedures for all adoptable pets. PAWS also offers behavioral support through its PAW UP Training Program to assist adopters in providing lasting homes for their new companions.

Learn More
Williamson County Regional Animal Shelter

The Williamson County Regional Animal Shelter (WCRAS) is a collaborative, no-kill facility serving Williamson County and the cities of Round Rock, Cedar Park, Hutto, and Leander. It provides shelter and care for lost, abandoned, and displaced animals, aiming to increase pet adoptions and reunite lost pets with their families. WCRAS offers adoption services, foster programs, and community initiatives like the Best Match program to ensure successful pet placements.

Learn More
Love And Puppy Paws Dog Rescue (LAPP Dog Rescue)

Love And Puppy Paws Dog Rescue (LAPP Dog Rescue) is a 100% foster-based, nonprofit organization dedicated to rescuing dogs from overpopulated shelters in Central and South Texas, including Austin and Corpus Christi. They accept dogs of all breeds, ages, and medical conditions, providing them with care in volunteer foster homes until they are ready for adoption. LAPP Dog Rescue emphasizes thoughtful matching between dogs and families, ensuring each dog finds a loving, permanent home.
